Everything ties at 2 (the `+2` for being a function), and `printc` wins because it's first. Three concrete bugs in `extractSymbolHints` + `scoreSymbol`:

1. **Verb words like "delete" become hints** but they shouldn't be matched against symbols.
2. **No fuzzy/substring matching when nothing exact matches** — `draw_pixel` should fuzzy-match `put_pixel` (shared `pixel`) or `draw_*` (shared `draw_`) substantially better than `printc`.
3. **The simple word-split loop** adds `draw_pixel` itself as a hint, but `qn.includes("draw_pixel")` finds nothing and it falls through. The `n.includes(h) || h.includes(n)` rule never fires because no name/qualified name contains the full string.
4. **Tie-breaker is unstable** (first match wins) — should fail loudly when ambiguous.

Fix plan:
- Add a stopword list for verbs.
- When the user-typed identifier contains `_`, also score against its **components** (`draw`, `pixel`) and against the symbol name's components. A symbol that shares a component should score much higher than an unrelated one.
- If the top candidate's score margin over second-place is small AND no exact match exists, throw a "could not find" error suggesting candidates instead of silently picking the wrong one.
